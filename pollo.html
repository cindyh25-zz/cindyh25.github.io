2. Onboarding üö¢

We wanted to create an onboarding flow for the first time a user makes a poll to make sure they know of all options. Particularly, that
  • Starting a question without inputting anything will autofill with choices "A", "B", etc.
  • Selecting an option marks it as a correct answer.
  • You can either save the question to poll later (planning lectures in advance) or start polling on the spot.
Here is the final prototype.
Here is how I got there.

First, I mapped out different orderings and lengths of the onboarding flow to mimic how a professor would make a poll themselves and make it easy to follow.

I decided on Flow 3 because it was the most comprehensive, highlighting all features without unecessary steps. It also presented the autofill option more as a secondary option to filling out each question. I chose this because prior research showed the primary use case is for professors planning out questions in advance rather than on the spot. The autofill is still important if professors poll spontaneously or have questions on the lecture slides that don't fit within our length/format constraints to display on the app.

Visual explorations

I also explored how to visually highlight both the specific parts of the screen (buttons, text fields, etc.) and the text for onboarding instructions. I used a gray overlay and white outlines and text to emphasize components and provide context.

Option A is more dynamic which brings users' attention to the buttons highlighted. But, the copy is less direct and the motion does not leave a good space for a "tap to continue" prompt.
Option B makes the text the primary focus with a heavier weight, fixed position, and simple copy.

I chose A based on feedback that the moving instructions provides clearer focus on where to click and how to navigate.

4. Designing Pollo for web üíª

To address professor's desire for more group controls and hesitance to allow phones in class, we are currently working on building a Pollo web app.

Our main goal for a web platform is to remove the friction with group controls and exporting attendance that professors faced through the smaller mobile interface. This semester, we stripped away many group controls in order to ship a better functioning MVP. For web, these are our initial feature requirements in addition to the functionality of the mobile app.

  • More natural interface to draft lecture questions ahead of time
  • Seamless navigation between Pollo and exporting attendance as a CSV
  • Customization and group controls
    • Control over how to display results (i.e. displaying percentages with vs. without the answer)
    • Setting location boundaries for where students can participate in the poll (for attendance purposes)
    • Customization of scoring and participation settings (i.e. making certain questions worth more credit)
